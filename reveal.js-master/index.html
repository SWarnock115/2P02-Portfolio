Virtual Boy

By: Sydnee Warnock

What is it?

  • One of the first attempt at Virtual Reality gaming
  • Developed by Nintendo in the late 1980's and early 1990's
  • Most famous for its commercial and critical failure

What Happened?

  • The Price
  • The Marketing
  • The Design

The Bad - Price

  • $200 vs $60

The Bad - Design

  • Red on Black
  • Caused eye strain, back strain and nausea

The Bad - Marketing

  • Reportedly $25 million spent on marketing
  • Targeted the adults
  • Allowed the console to be rented at BlockBuster

Tech Details

  • Nintendo's first 32-bit system, the N64 came right after.
  • Uses a pair of linear arrays in conjunction with flat oscillating mirrors to track the users eye field

Hardware

  • Display Unit
  • Stand
  • Controller (Also the power supply)

Graphics and Display

  • Monochromatic Colouring
  • Attempted to create 3D images using parallax effect
  • Together they created the eye strain and nausea reported by many users

Games

  • Only 22 games were released, only 14 reached NA

Media Archaeology

Humanity loves moving forward especially when it comes to media. We have pushed the boundaries of engagement further and further each generation., First time it was the radio, after that it was cinema, the next step is/was virtual reality. The desire to consume media has pushed us to greater heights then ever thought possible. A hundred years ago people were running out of theaters because they thought a train was actually going to come through the screen, now we have headsets that place us inside a game. The Virtual Boy was attempting to push forward media once again, yet sadly it failed.

Failure

The failure of the Virtual Boy tells us a lot about our society. Despite the desire to always push forward with innovation these advancements can still be held back by simple things like comfort and looks. The Virtual Boy, despite its lofty desires failed at very basic things that are vital for an everyday person. By creating such an uncomfortable tool they greatly diminished the impact it would have. Humanity has spent generations in their pursuit of comfort and any attempt to go forward must understand this and include it into the tool, otherwise it will fail just like the Virtual Boy.

Alternative History

What would have happened if the Virtual Boy had not failed? I want to look at a potential future that may have occurred if the the Virtual Boy had not failed right out of the gates. The Virtual Boy was released in 1995, the next virtual reality console was released almost two decades later. If the virtual reality console boom had not burst soon after it started, we could have had two decades worth of consumers playing and testing out new virtual reality consoles. Those two decades could have fostered an immense amount of research and creation, maybe even pushed us into Ready Player One level of virtual reality

Zombie Tech

As previously mentioned virtual reality has recently made a comeback. This raising of dead technology is important as it shows that innovation can be found in the past and even its failures. This zombie tech is seeing new life pumped into it and hopefully this time around, it will reach it original goal and please the audience. Another example of the Virtual Boy dead tech coming back to life is found in the 3DS, it uses the same parallax effect that the original console used in its attempt to create a 3D field of view, minus the constant nausea and eye strain.

What it Teaches Us

So what does this all teach us? It teaches us that innovation is second to comfort and looks. That despite its ambitious goals it most still cater to the whims of the consumers and that ignoring something simple can lead to a products downfall It teaches us that that the future is fickle and can be changed by one event. It teaches us, that despite failing, there is still hope for dead products to come back to life in new and exciting ways. We can learn from the failure of the Virtual Boy so in the future, the next step forward is not stymied.
